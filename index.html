Who We Are

.. trying to know what seismograms tell

We study the sources of large and small earthquakes occurring at home and abroad, including the 2016 scale 5.8 Gyeongju earthquake and 2017 scale 5.4 Pohang earthquake. Using the seismic observation data obtained by directly operating a high-density broadband seismic observation network, the characteristics of the seismic sources associated with these earthquakes, such as the range and posture of the underground fault motion that caused the Gyeongju and Pohang earthquakes, could be revealed in great detail.

We welcome students interested in seismological research.

Thank you.

What We Do

... trying to find and dig seismograms

We are very interested in using seismic waves to reveal the crust and mantle structures of East Asia including Korea. Cross-correlation analysis of the background noise of continuous seismic records recorded from the seismic network of the southern Korean peninsula and the seismic network of neighboring China and Japan, and the seismic wave tomography method were applied to the detailed crust structure of the lower part of the Korean Peninsula and the seismic wave velocity structure of the crust and upper mantle of the lower part of East Asia. Was imaged.

Recently (October 2020), a joint study was initiated with the World Heritage Headquarters in Jeju Special Self-Governing Province and Seoul National University/Korea University to investigate the magma structure, which is the root of Jeju volcanic bodies. Five broadband seismometers have been installed on the summit of Hallasan Mountain and at a height of about 1,500 m around it, and geophones are being installed at about 70 locations with auxiliary observation networks. It is expected to be able to visualize the three-dimensional structure of the lower part of the volcanic body in Jeju Island by observing micro-earthquakes occurring in the lower part of Jeju Island and analyzing seismic waves from earthquakes occurring in a distance.

Where We Go

... trying to get where seismograms are

Modern seismology is observational science. Pukyong National University's Seismology & Geophysics Laboratory is leading research to further investigate the characteristics of earthquake sources and the internal structure of the Earth through extensive and detailed earthquake observations. We operate high-performance seismic observation equipment and real-time and precise analysis computer servers, and conduct larger and more detailed research in cooperation with related seismology research groups at home and abroad. The ultimate goal of these studies is to create a society that is safe from earthquake disasters, starting with a fundamental search for natural phenomena.
